Why Acne Happens (Especially in Kenya)

Hormones

Puberty, periods, stress & conditions like PCOS (very common triggers here.)

Humidity & Pollution

Nairobi/Mombasa air + heat makes oil production go crazy therefore more clogged pores.

Diet & Habits

Heavy oils, dairy, sugary snacks can worsen it for many.

Bacteria & Clogged Pores

Dead skin + excess oil = perfect home for acne-causing bacteria.

Common Acne Myths in Kenya- Busted!

Myth: Acne means your skin is dirty

Truth: It's mostly hormones & oil, not poor hygiene. Over-washing can actually make it worse.

Myth: Only teenagers get acne

Truth: Many adults (especially women 20 to 40) struggle with it too.

Myth: Popping pimples helps

Truth: It spreads bacteria, more acne + dark scars (hyperpigmentation is a big issue on our skin tones).

Myth: Oily skin doesn't need moisturizer

Truth: Skipping it makes skin produce even more oil.

Simple Daily Routine That Works in Kenya

Keep it minimal: gentle cleansing, hydration, one targeted treatment, and sun protection. Start slow, patch-test actives, and be consistent—most see clearer skin in 6–12 weeks.

?? Morning (2–3 minutes)

  1. Wash your face
    Use a gentle face wash. Don’t scrub hard. Just massage it in for about 30 seconds and rinse.
    If you’re oily or get clogged pores, a face wash with a little salicylic acid can help.
    (Try: CeraVe Foaming Cleanser or La Roche-Posay Effaclar – available at MYDAWA/Goodlife)
  2. Moisturize
    Yes, even if you have acne. Skipping moisturizer can actually make your skin produce more oil.
    Pick something light and non-greasy.
    (Try: CeraVe PM Facial Moisturizing Lotion or Neutrogena Hydro Boost Gel-Cream)
  3. Sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher)
    This prevents dark marks from getting worse and protects healing skin.
    If you hate sunscreen, look for a lightweight or matte one.
    (Try: La Roche-Posay Anthelios Oil Control or Eucerin Oil Control – matte & non-greasy for humid weather)
    That’s it for the morning.

?? Night (3–5 minutes)

  1. Wash your face
    Same cleanser as morning.
  2. Apply ONE acne treatment
    Don’t use everything at once. Choose one:
    • Salicylic acid ? good for clogged pores and blackheads
      (e.g., The Ordinary Salicylic Acid 2% Solution or CeraVe SA Smoothing Cleanser)
    • Benzoyl peroxide ? good for red, angry pimples
      (e.g., PanOxyl 4% or CeraVe Acne Foaming Cream Cleanser – start low % to avoid dryness)
    • Adapalene ? good for long-term acne control
      (e.g., Differin Gel 0.1% – OTC in many Kenyan pharmacies/online)
    Start using it 3–4 nights a week. If your skin handles it well, you can use it more often.
  3. Moisturize again
    If your skin feels dry or irritated, apply moisturizer before and after your treatment (this helps reduce irritation).
    Use the same lightweight one as morning.

Patch test new products. Introduce one active at a time. Be patient — real change takes time. If breakouts are severe, cystic, or scarring, see a dermatologist (e.g., Avané Clinic, Este Medical).
